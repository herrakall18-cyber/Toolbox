Why WHtR matters more than BMI

Waist‑to‑height ratio (WHtR) captures central adiposity — the fat stored around your organs. Unlike BMI, which can misclassify muscular individuals or those with hidden visceral fat, WHtR directly reflects abdominal obesity. Research shows that a WHtR above 0.5 is linked to elevated cardiovascular risk, type 2 diabetes, and metabolic syndrome, often outperforming BMI in predicting health outcomes. It’s simple: keeping your waist circumference below half your height is a robust target for most adults.

To use this waist to height ratio calculator, just enter your waist and height in centimeters. The WHtR calculator instantly computes the ratio and indicates your risk category. For example, a 175 cm person with a 87 cm waist has a WHtR of 0.497 — inside the healthy zone. The waist height ratio calculator is especially useful for children, older adults, and diverse ethnicities, where BMI alone can be misleading. Many healthcare professionals now consider WHtR a superior screening tool.

This free tool uses the same cutoff validated in large studies: WHtR < 0.5 → low risk, 0.5‑0.59 → increased risk, 0.6+ → high risk. Use it alongside your doctor’s advice to monitor changes over time.
