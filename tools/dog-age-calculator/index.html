Why the old “multiply by 7” rule doesn’t work

For decades, pet owners relied on the simple rule that one dog year equals seven human years. However, veterinary research has shown that this rule significantly oversimplifies how dogs age. Dogs mature much faster in their first two years, and the aging rate then depends strongly on their size. Small breeds tend to age more slowly than large or giant breeds, meaning the same chronological age can produce very different “human age” equivalents.

The calculator above uses the widely accepted veterinary formula: the first year of a dog’s life equals 15 human years (regardless of size). The second year adds about 9 human years. After that, each subsequent year adds a size-dependent multiplier. For small dogs it’s roughly 4 human years per dog year, for medium 5, for large 6, and for giant breeds around 7–8 human years per year. These values come from recent studies on canine aging and are recommended by many veterinarians for a more accurate estimate.

Using this dog age calculator you can quickly find out how old is my dog in human years — simply enter your dog’s age and select the breed size. Whether you have a Chihuahua or a Great Dane, the dog years to human years conversion now reflects real biological aging. Keep in mind that individual health, genetics, and lifestyle also play a role, but this tool gives you a reliable baseline for understanding your companion’s life stage.
