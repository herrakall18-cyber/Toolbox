How to use the boiled egg timer

Whether you’re wondering how long to boil eggs for a soft, dippy yolk or a firm hard-boiled egg, this calculator takes the guesswork out of the process. Simply choose your egg size (small, medium, large, or extra large), tell us if they’re straight from the fridge or at room temperature, and select your preferred doneness. The tool instantly gives you the precise boiling time needed — no more undercooked or rubbery eggs.

Why egg size and starting temperature matter

A fridge-cold large egg needs about 30–60 seconds longer than a room‑temperature egg to reach the same consistency. Egg size also changes the heat penetration: smaller eggs cook faster, while extra‑large eggs require a bit more time. Our boiled egg timer accounts for both variables, so you can reliably get the perfect texture every time — whether you’re meal-prepping hard-boiled eggs or serving soft-boiled eggs with toast soldiers.

Tips for perfect boiled eggs

For best results, gently lower eggs into already boiling water, then reduce to a simmer. Use a timer (like the one above) and immediately transfer eggs to an ice bath when done to stop the cooking. This tool is designed for large, standard chicken eggs; if you use duck or quail eggs, adjust expectations.
