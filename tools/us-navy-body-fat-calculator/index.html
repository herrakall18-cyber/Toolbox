How the navy body fat calculator works — This body fat percentage calculator uses the U.S. Navy circumference-based formula, originally developed for military personnel. It estimates lean mass and fat mass from just three (or four) simple tape measurements: height, neck, waist, and for women, hip circumference. The method is widely used because it's fast, non-invasive, and reasonably accurate for most body types.

Why choose this method? Unlike bioelectrical impedance or calipers, the navy body fat calculator only requires a measuring tape and a few minutes. It correlates well with DEXA and hydrostatic weighing in controlled studies, especially for individuals with moderate body fat. Use it weekly to track trends — consistency in measurement technique matters more than a single number.

Tips for accuracy: Measure neck just below the larynx (Adam's apple), waist at navel level for men and at the narrowest point for women, and hips at the widest point. Keep the tape snug but not compressing the skin. All measurements should be in inches. The calculator automatically adjusts formulas based on gender.
