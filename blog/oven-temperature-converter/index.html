Oven Temperature Converter: Because 180°C doesn’t mean much to an American oven

Posted by the ToolsForUse team · April 2025

Last weekend I tried to bake a loaf of soda bread from an Irish recipe. It called for 200°C (fan), which my brain immediately blanks on. I know 350°F is sort of "medium," and 400°F is "hot," but 200? That’s not even on my oven dial. I ended up guessing, setting it to 390°F, and the bread came out with a crust like a hockey puck. Edible, but not what I was aiming for.

That’s a small thing, but it’s also the kind of friction that makes you hesitate before using a recipe from another country. And it happens all the time—British baking books, French pastry blogs, Australian casserole sites. They all use Celsius, and some still use gas marks. I don’t want to open a separate tab and search "200 C to F" every time, especially when the results are full of ads or show a single conversion without gas mark options.

Why not just use Google?

You can, and it works okay for a quick number. But if you're like me, you’ve also run into the problem of "fan" vs "conventional" ovens, or you’re following a recipe that lists gas mark 6 and you have to do a second lookup. And the Google result doesn’t show you the equivalent gas mark unless you scroll. It’s not hard, but it’s a little annoying when you’re mid-recipe with flour on your hands.

There are plenty of converter widgets out there, but a lot of them are either buried in blog posts, require JavaScript to load, or show you a table that’s too small to read on a phone. I wanted something stripped down: you put in a temperature, it shows you all three formats at once. No extra clicks, no ads for air fryers, just the numbers.

What the tool actually does

It’s a single input where you type a number (or pick from common baking temps), and it instantly shows you the Fahrenheit, Celsius (both conventional and fan-assisted), and gas mark. If you enter, say, 350°F, it tells you that’s about 175°C conventional or 155°C fan, and gas mark 4. If you put in 200°C, you get 400°F and gas mark 6. It also has a small set of common presets for things like "slow oven" or "hot oven" so you don’t even need to type if you’re just checking a rough range.

It’s designed to be usable on a phone while you’re holding a spatula, and it works without any fancy loading or trackers. No login, no email required—just the conversion.
