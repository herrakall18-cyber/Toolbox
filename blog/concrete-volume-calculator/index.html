Why I built a concrete volume calculator (and made it public)

by the guy behind ToolsForUse · April 2025

Last summer I poured a small slab for a garden shed. I measured the hole, did the math on my phone, got a number, rounded up a little because I was paranoid, and ordered the concrete. I ended up with almost a full yard left over. Had to pay extra disposal fees, spent a day figuring out where to dump it, and the whole thing just bugged me. I’m not a contractor, I just didn’t want to run short — and in trying to be safe, I overcorrected.

A few months later I helped a friend with a footing for a small retaining wall. This time I lost the sketch, recalculated three times, still wasn’t sure, called the ready-mix place and they asked for cubic yards. I froze. I gave them an estimate, ended up a half-yard short, and had to buy a second mixer load that was barely a quarter full. Waste of time, money, and embarrassing.

What’s out there already

Plenty of concrete calculators exist. But honestly, most of them are either:

  • Buried inside a home improvement retailer’s site with pop-ups and product recommendations,
  • So bare-bones they only handle a simple rectangle (no columns, no round footings),
  • Or they ask for inches, feet, and weird unit combos that make you convert in your head anyway.

This isn’t a dig at those – they’re fine if you already know the formula and just need a button. But I wanted something that handled the three shapes I actually encounter (slab, column, footing), did the math consistently in cubic yards (which is what the concrete yard uses), and gave me a number I could trust without second-guessing.

What the tool actually does

You pick a shape — slab (rectangle), column (cylinder), or footing (rectangular base). You enter dimensions in feet and inches, or just feet with decimals. The calculator converts everything to cubic yards and shows the exact number. No rounding baked in, no “add 10% for waste” (that’s your call). You get a clean decimal you can tell the dispatcher.

I also added a tiny visual summary so you know you entered the right numbers — helps catch fat-finger mistakes before you order. Nothing fancy, just a sanity check.

Honest closing

This isn’t going to change your life. It’s a focused calculator that does one thing without fluff. If you’re a DIYer, a small contractor, or just someone who’s tired of guessing and then wasting money on extra material, it might save you a headache. I use it myself. That’s really the whole pitch.
