Recipe Scaler Calculator

The one that actually lets you cook for 3 (or 11) without math brain.

I ruined a batch of chili last month. The recipe was for 6 servings; I needed to feed 10. I doubled everything by rote — but the beans, the broth, the spices? They didn't scale evenly once I accounted for evaporation and pot size. That was my fault. But the bigger problem was even getting to that point: I had to grab my phone, open the calculator app, and do 8 separate multiplication problems. For one recipe. By the time I finished, my onions were burning.

It sounds trivial, but when you're mid-recipe, hands messy, doubling or halving every ingredient is a genuine friction point. If you cook much, you've been there: you either risk winging it (and end up with bland or oversalted food) or you do a bunch of tiny math problems and lose your flow.

Why not just use a normal calculator (or a spreadsheet)?

Sure, you can do it manually. But:

  • Mental overhead. You have to keep track of which ingredients you've already adjusted, especially if there are 10+ lines. One slip and your cumin is double or your salt is half.
  • Fractions suck. "1 ⅓ cups" times 1.5 is not something you want to do while holding a spatula.
  • Units. Most calculators don't keep the unit (cup, tsp, g) attached — you have to remember it yourself.
  • Existing "recipe scalers" are often over-engineered. I tried a few. They want you to create an account, or they try to parse natural language ("2 cups flour") and fail when you write "2 c. flour, sifted". Or they just display a table with no way to copy or print.

I wanted something that felt like a small kitchen tool: immediate, humble, and exactly one function.

What this one does

You paste or type your ingredient list (one per line, quantity + unit + ingredient), tell it the original servings and your desired servings, and it spits out the scaled list. That's it. It preserves the ratio of every ingredient — if the original recipe was balanced, the scaled version will be too. No sign-up, no parsing magic that fails on egg counts or "to taste" notes. It just multiplies the numbers and keeps the text.

  • Works with decimals, fractions (like ½ or 1 ⅓), and whole numbers.
  • Keeps your unit and ingredient name as-is.
  • You can copy the output with one click.

I built it for myself, but I figured others might be in the same boat. So I cleaned it up and put it on ToolsForUse.
